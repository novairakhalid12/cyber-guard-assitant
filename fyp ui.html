Legal Information

Pakistan Cybercrime Laws

The Prevention of Electronic Crimes Act (PECA) 2016 is the primary legislation dealing with cybercrimes in Pakistan. Key provisions include:

  • Section 10: Cyber terrorism - Up to 14 years imprisonment or fine up to Rs. 50 million
  • Section 11: Hate speech - Up to 7 years imprisonment or fine up to Rs. 10 million
  • Section 21: Offences against dignity of a natural person - Up to 3 years imprisonment or fine up to Rs. 1 million
  • Section 24: Cyberstalking - Up to 3 years imprisonment or fine up to Rs. 1 million

For more detailed information, consult the complete PECA 2016 document or seek legal advice.

Your Rights as a Victim

As a victim of cybercrime in Pakistan, you have the right to:

  • File a complaint with the Federal Investigation Agency (FIA)
  • Request anonymity during the investigation process
  • Seek legal counsel and representation
  • Receive updates on the status of your case
  • Protection from retaliation or further harassment
